Tiramisu

Ingredients

For the cream:

  • 3 large eggs, separated
  • 100g (½ cup) granulated sugar
  • 250g (1 cup) mascarpone cheese
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)

For the layers:

  • 200g (about 20-24) ladyfinger biscuits (Savoiardi)
  • 300ml (1¼ cups) strong espresso coffee, cooled
  • 2 tablespoons coffee liqueur (like Kahlúa or Marsala wine) (optional)

For dusting:

  • Unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting

Instructions:

1. Prepare the mascarpone cream:

  • In a medium bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until thick and pale (you can use an electric mixer for this, about 3-5 minutes).
  • Gently fold in the mascarpone cheese and vanilla extract (if using) until smooth. Be careful not to overmix.

2. Whip the egg whites:

  • In a separate bowl, whisk the egg whites until stiff peaks form.
  • Carefully fold the whipped egg whites into the mascarpone mixture. Do this in batches, folding gently so as not to deflate the egg whites. You should get a light, airy cream.

3. Prepare the coffee dip:

  • In a shallow dish, combine the cooled espresso and coffee liqueur (if using).

4. Assemble the tiramisu:

  • Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee mixture (just a second or two, so they don’t become too soggy) and line them in the bottom of a rectangular dish (about 8x8 inches).
  • Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the soaked ladyfingers, smoothing it out with a spatula.
  • Add another layer of coffee-dipped ladyfingers on top.
  • Spread the remaining mascarpone cream over the second layer of ladyfingers.

5. Chill the tiramisu:

  • Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours (or overnight) to allow the flavors to meld and the dessert to set.

6. Dust and serve:

  • Before serving, generously dust the top with unsweetened cocoa powder using a fine sieve.
  • You can also grate a bit of dark chocolate on top for extra indulgence.

Enjoy!
